This Nigerian Beans porridge recipe is simple and loaded with nourishing ingredients that take that plain ole black-eyed peas to a whole new level. Don’t believe me? Try it out and see

    I was contemplating keeping my beans porridge recipe a secret but I got way too excited after receiving the most wonderful feedback from my friends. I guess my head was swelling and I couldn’t bear leaving you out of the mix.

    Prep time: 20 mins

    Cook time: 1hr 20mins

    How to make Nigerian Beans

    Apart from all this deliciousness, you should expect large flavours! Savouriness from the spices, natural sweetness from the vegetable base and plantains- MA PA MI Naw! If your beans porridge is made well you WON’T BE ABLE TO STOP! You’ll be back for seconds, thirds, fourths and let’s hope the pot isn’t gone by the time your guests arrive

    Spices

    • 2 tbsp Crayfish powder
    • 1/2 tbsp thyme
    • 1/2 tbsp paprika

    Ingredients

    • 4 cups Black eyed beans
    • 2 Red bell peppers
    • 1/2 cups palm oil
    • 3 tomatoes
    • 2 ripe plantains chopped
    • 11/2 onion sliced
    • 2 red scotch bonnets
    • 3 smoked turkey wings

    SPICES

    • salt to taste
    • 3 tbsp crayfish
    • 2 tsp Cameroon pepper
    • 1 tbsp iru locust beans
    • 2 stock cubes

    Instructions
    1. Parboil beans in pot for 20 mins. Drain the water, wash lightly and add in new water
    2. In the pot of beans add half an onion (sliced) and 1tbsp of salt and boil for 20 minutes. Then add plantains and boil for 10 more minutes . Once cooked drain excess water
    3. In another pot add palm oil, saute onions. Once onions are browned pour in pepper base and season with 1 stock cube and crayfish
    4. Chop smoked turkey and place it in the sauce to cook for a few minutes. Then, slowly transfer your sauce to the beans, stir well and cook for 5 minutes on medium heat
    5. Taste to check for seasoning and adjust spices accordingly. Turn off heat and allow it cool a bit
    6. Serve and enjoy
